# OMNIA-CONSTANT

Search for persistent structural constants across measurable systems using OMNIA and OMNIA-INVARIANCE.

---

## What this repository is

OMNIA-CONSTANT is not a separate theory.

It is the continuation of OMNIA-INVARIANCE.

OMNIA-INVARIANCE compares structural trajectories across domains.

OMNIA-CONSTANT asks whether any measurable relation remains persistent across those trajectories.

---

## Core chain

```text
OMNIA
= measure structure

OMNIA-INVARIANCE
= compare structural trajectories

OMNIA-CONSTANT
= test for persistent cross-domain invariants


---

What is a structural constant?

A structural constant is a measurable relation, trajectory, ratio, or invariant behavior that persists across:

transformations

perturbations

representations

scales

domains


It is not assumed.

It must be tested.


---

Candidate requirements

A candidate structural constant must:

survive perturbation

survive representation changes

survive scaling

remain distinguishable from noise

remain measurable across domains

remain statistically non-trivial

fail when tested against false patterns



---

What this repository does NOT claim

This repository does NOT claim:

a final theory of reality

a universal physical constant

proof of consciousness

proof of intelligence

semantic understanding

causal certainty

predictive certainty

equivalence between domains


The repository searches for candidates.

It does not declare final constants.


---

Experimental posture

OMNIA-CONSTANT is:

exploratory

experimental

falsifiable

cross-domain

measurement-first


A candidate constant is valid only if it survives stress testing.


---

Failure condition

If a candidate invariant cannot be separated from random noise, baseline artifacts, representation effects, or metric bias, then it is rejected.

No candidate is protected from failure.


---

Relationship with OMNIA

OMNIA measures structural behavior under transformation.

It produces local structural signals such as stability, instability, degradation, recovery, collapse, or persistence.


---

Relationship with OMNIA-INVARIANCE

OMNIA-INVARIANCE compares those signals as trajectories.

It studies curve shape, position, divergence, collapse timing, topology, and cross-domain alignment.


---

Purpose of OMNIA-CONSTANT

OMNIA-CONSTANT exists to test whether some relations remain stable after both layers:

structure measurement
↓
trajectory comparison
↓
persistent invariant testing

The central question is:

Does anything measurable remain structurally persistent across domains?


---

Claim boundary

Structural persistence does not imply ontological equivalence.

Two domains may show similar invariant behavior while having completely different internal mechanisms.

OMNIA-CONSTANT only tests measurable structural persistence.

Interpretation remains external.


---

Core principle

measurement != interpretation != decision


---

Current status

Initial repository.

No structural constant is declared yet.

The first goal is to define candidate criteria, failure tests, and baseline comparisons before accepting any invariant as meaningful.
