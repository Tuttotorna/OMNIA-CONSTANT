# CANDIDATE TYPES

## Purpose

Not all persistent structures are numeric constants.

OMNIA-CONSTANT classifies multiple forms of possible structural persistence across domains.

This document defines the current candidate categories.

---

# TYPE-A · Ratio Invariants

Stable measurable ratios that persist across transformations or domains.

Examples:

- scaling ratios
- stability/instability ratios
- collapse/recovery ratios
- persistence/entropy ratios

Candidate requirement:

```text
ratio remains approximately stable under perturbation


---

TYPE-B · Trajectory Invariants

Persistent trajectory behaviors observed across different systems.

Examples:

similar collapse curves

convergence patterns

saturation dynamics

recovery geometries

divergence timing


Candidate requirement:

trajectory shape remains structurally comparable
across domains and representations


---

TYPE-C · Topology Invariants

Persistent structural organization independent of representation details.

Examples:

connectivity persistence

graph robustness

cluster behavior

transition topology

structural continuity


Candidate requirement:

topological structure survives representation changes


---

TYPE-D · Collapse Invariants

Persistent behaviors observed during degradation or failure.

Examples:

collapse timing

critical transition geometry

instability acceleration

irreversible transition signatures

saturation before failure


Candidate requirement:

collapse dynamics remain distinguishable from noise


---

TYPE-E · Transition Invariants

Persistent signatures during state transitions.

Examples:

phase-transition-like behavior

bifurcation patterns

transition asymmetry

directional instability

recovery hysteresis


Candidate requirement:

transition signatures persist under perturbation


---

TYPE-F · Persistence Invariants

Structures that remain measurable despite domain variation.

Examples:

long-lived coherence

repeated structural motifs

stable cross-domain signatures

invariant structural residues


Candidate requirement:

signal survives cross-domain stress testing


---

TYPE-G · Noise-Separation Invariants

Persistent ability to distinguish structure from random behavior.

Examples:

structure vs noise separation

false-pattern rejection

adversarial robustness

random baseline divergence


Candidate requirement:

candidate remains separable from controlled randomness


---

Important boundary

A candidate invariant is not automatically meaningful.

Persistence alone does not imply:

causality

universality

physical law

semantic truth

ontological equivalence


The framework only measures structural persistence.

Interpretation remains external.


---

Core principle

measurement != interpretation != decision


---

Current status

This taxonomy is provisional.

New candidate categories may emerge or existing categories may collapse under testing.
