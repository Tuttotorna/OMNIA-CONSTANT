# TRANSITION REGION

## Purpose

OMNIA-CONSTANT does not currently treat `Ω≈0.6` as an exact universal constant.

The current hypothesis is weaker and more structural:

```text
Ω≈0.6 may represent a transition region
between instability and persistent structure.

This document defines the provisional transition-region framework.


---

Core idea

Instead of:

Ω = exact constant

OMNIA-CONSTANT studies:

critical persistence regions

The focus is not the exact number.

The focus is the structural transition behavior.


---

Provisional regions

R0 · Instability Region

Approximate range:

Ω << 0.6

Typical properties:

weak persistence

high volatility

rapid collapse

random-like behavior

low structural survival

unstable trajectories


Possible interpretation:

structure fails to persist


---

R1 · Transition Region

Approximate range:

Ω ≈ 0.55–0.65

Typical properties:

marginal persistence

unstable stability

partial structural survival

sensitivity to perturbation

mixed collapse/recovery behavior

boundary accumulation


Possible interpretation:

system approaches minimal non-trivial persistence

This region is the main focus of current OMNIA-CONSTANT experiments.


---

R2 · Persistence Region

Approximate range:

Ω >> 0.6

Typical properties:

stronger persistence

structural robustness

lower collapse sensitivity

higher trajectory stability

stronger invariant survival


Possible interpretation:

structure survives perturbation more consistently


---

Important distinction

These regions are provisional.

They are not assumed to be universal.

The regions may:

shift

overlap

collapse

fragment

split into subregions

disappear under deeper testing



---

Transition dynamics

The main research question is:

How do systems move between instability and persistence?

Key dynamics include:

collapse acceleration

recovery behavior

persistence drift

hysteresis

transition asymmetry

topology deformation

variance explosion

boundary clustering



---

Boundary interpretation

The transition region may represent:

minimal structural survival

critical persistence threshold

phase-transition-like behavior

instability/persistence crossover

emergence of non-trivial organization


Or it may simply be:

a metric artifact

a normalization artifact

a scale effect

a finite-sample illusion


All interpretations remain falsifiable.


---

Critical warning

A repeated boundary region is not automatically meaningful.

Transition regions can emerge artificially from:

preprocessing

metric design

compression effects

smoothing

normalization

hidden correlations

representation bias


The burden of proof remains on the candidate.


---

Current working hypothesis

Persistent systems may naturally cluster near or above
a critical Ω transition region,
while unstable systems remain below it.

This hypothesis is experimental only.


---

Failure condition

Reject the transition-region hypothesis if:

noise produces indistinguishable regions

adversarial systems reproduce the same geometry

regions shift unpredictably

regions collapse under metric substitution

regions disappear across domains

regions fail replication



---

Core principle

measurement != interpretation != decision
