# Controlled Graph Perturbation Result

## Purpose

Determine whether the Ω≈0.6 corridor emerges when a known structure undergoes strong perturbation while still partially preserving identity.

This is the first fully controlled experiment in the OMNIA-CONSTANT investigation.

Unlike noisy real-world datasets, this experiment provides:

```text
- known structure
- known perturbation level
- measurable identity preservation
- measurable structural collapse

The central question:

Does Ω≈0.6 appear at the boundary
between preservation and destruction?


---

Experimental Design

Base structure

ring_lattice graph

Parameters:

{
  "nodes": 100,
  "k_neighbors": 4
}

The graph starts as a stable structured topology.


---

Controlled Perturbation

Perturbation levels:

0%
10%
20%
...
100%

At each level:

- edges removed
- edges rewired
- random edges injected

while measuring:

identity preservation

and:

Ω-like compatibility score


---

Key Idea

The experiment is designed around this principle:

Ω should become high when:
- change is significant
- identity is still partially preserved

Not:

pure order

and not:

pure chaos


---

Ω Definition Used

The experiment used:

Ω = 2 × change × preservation

Where:

change = perturbation level
preservation = residual structural identity

Interpretation:

Ω becomes large when:
- perturbation is strong
- but identity has not fully collapsed

This makes Ω a measure of:

compatible perturbation

rather than simple stability.
